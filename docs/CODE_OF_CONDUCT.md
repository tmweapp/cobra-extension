# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the COBRA project and its communities will be treated with respect and dignity.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Being gracious** in accepting constructive criticism
- **Focusing on what is best** for the community
- **Showing empathy** towards other community members
- **Respecting the time and effort** of contributors

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination based on any characteristic
- Offensive language or imagery
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Sharing others' private information without consent
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

---

## Enforcement

**Responsibilities:**

Project maintainers are responsible for:
- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to unacceptable behavior
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct

**Reporting:**

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by:
- Contacting the project maintainers at conduct@example.com
- Filing a report via our GitHub incident form
- Reaching out to a community moderator

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

**Consequences:**

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All project spaces (repositories, issue trackers, discussion boards)
- Official project events and gatherings
- Public spaces where an individual is representing the project
- When using official project email addresses or accounts
